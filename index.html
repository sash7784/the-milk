About Us

The Milk Resturant & Champagne Bar.

Khayelitsha's premium lifestyle entertainment boutique The Milk Restaurant & Champagne Bar has re-opened its doors after a 2-year break due to the COVID-19 global pandemic. The premium entertainment boutique was launched in 2018 by S'phelo Jalivane out of his pocket.He immediately was met with overwhelming success as the restaurant gained instant fame and prestige around Cape Town.Jalivane was however later on facing varying challenges, as a result, he had to take a break in 2020. “I started the business too soon. I was in a rush and wanted to get it off the ground, not knowing the challenges I would face with infrastructure. I took the kitchen equipment required and other appliances for granted, and this led to bad services. Additionally, I was in financial woes. I had invested every cent in construction, borrowed money from here and there and collectors were chasing me. I was in financial distress.

I had to face the music and own up to the many mistakes which had caught up with me. The lockdown was a blessing in disguise as I was drowning, but I did not want to give up on my dream.Now that I look at it that wasn't healthy. Lockdown forced me to sit and simmer in my reality. It saved me because through it I developed a healthy working business relationship with the two successful brothers and outdoor Mecca Kings.” Fast forward two years later, the Restaurant & Champagne Bar has relaunched with two new partners on tour, the Mbeki brothers, Mfundo and Mshayi, who are co-founders of yet another popular eatery, the Rands Cape Town. A relived Jalivane says he's very excited and looking forward to this new partnership and reunion as the three businessmen have collaborated on other projects before The partnership is an important step in this township as it signifies the importance of unity and the development of the community's economy. "This is a great opportunity to show what we can do when we work together as brothers, helping each other grow in business.This partnership means a lot for the township as it also signifies that we are owning the spaces where we live.

It's important to do whatever it takes to protect Kasi businesses, ikasi for ikasi,” said Mfundo. The premium lifestyle entertainment boutique is divided into three categories; the ground would serve as a full-time liquor store with a separate entrance to the restaurant.The restaurant and champagne bar is on the first floor with a primary focus on cuisine, offering semi-fine dining as opposed to its initial dining experience. Guests can expect to enjoy breakfast, lunch, and dinner while seated in modern and stylish furniture with velvet influences> Dish offering ranges from Seafood, grills, and light meals.The rooftop offers an overview of the Khayelitsha landscape and is designed with a high-end clientele in mind, who enjoy their premium beverages such as champagne, cognac and whisky. The Lounge setting is 4 -10-seater lounges to accommodate groups.The Milk Restaurant & Champagne Bar employs about 35 Khayelitsha residents and is located on the corner of Makabeni Road and Zantsi Street in Khayelitsha and opens from Monday to Sunday from 10 am to 11 pm.
